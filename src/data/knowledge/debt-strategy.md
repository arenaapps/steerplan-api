# Debt Repayment Strategies

## Avalanche Method (Mathematically Optimal)

Pay minimums on all debts, then put extra money toward the **highest interest rate** debt first.

- Saves the most money in total interest paid.
- Best for: disciplined savers who want to minimise cost.

## Snowball Method (Psychologically Motivating)

Pay minimums on all debts, then put extra money toward the **smallest balance** first.

- Quick wins build momentum and motivation.
- Best for: people who need visible progress to stay motivated.

## Key Principles

- Always pay at least the minimum on every debt — missed payments damage your credit score.
- Prioritise high-interest debt (credit cards, payday loans) over low-interest debt (student loans, mortgages).
- Consider balance transfer cards (0% interest for 12–24 months) for credit card debt — but pay it off before the promotional period ends.
- Student loan repayments in the UK are income-contingent and written off after 25–40 years. Don't overpay unless you'll actually clear the balance.

## Debt Warning Signs

- Using credit cards for essentials (food, bills)
- Only paying minimums each month
- Borrowing to repay other debts
- Debt-to-income ratio above 40%
