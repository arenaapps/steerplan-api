# UK Pension Rules 2025/26

Pensions are the most tax-efficient way to save for retirement in the UK. Contributions get tax relief, and the pot grows tax-free.

## Annual Allowance

- **Standard annual allowance:** £60,000 per year (or 100% of earnings, whichever is lower).
- **Carry forward:** Unused allowance from the previous 3 tax years can be carried forward.
- **Money Purchase Annual Allowance (MPAA):** £10,000 — applies once you start flexibly accessing your pension.
- **Tapered Annual Allowance:** If adjusted income exceeds £260,000, allowance tapers by £1 for every £2 above, down to a minimum of £10,000.

## Tax Relief

- Basic rate taxpayers: 20% relief added automatically (contribute £80, get £100 in your pot).
- Higher rate: Claim additional 20% via self-assessment (effective cost of £60 for £100 in pot).
- Additional rate: Claim additional 25% via self-assessment (effective cost of £55 for £100 in pot).

## Accessing Your Pension

- **Minimum age:** 55 (rising to 57 from April 2028).
- **25% tax-free lump sum:** Up to 25% of your pot can be withdrawn tax-free.
- **Drawdown:** Withdraw flexibly, paying income tax on amounts above the tax-free portion.
- **Annuity:** Buy a guaranteed income for life.

## State Pension

- **Full new State Pension:** £221.20 per week (2025/26).
- Need 35 qualifying years of NI contributions for full amount.
- Minimum 10 qualifying years to get anything.
- State Pension age: currently 66, rising to 67 by 2028 and 68 by 2046.

## Workplace Pension

- Auto-enrolment minimum: 8% total (5% employee + 3% employer).
- Employer contributions are essentially free money — always contribute at least enough to get the full employer match.
