# Emergency Fund Guidelines

An emergency fund is your financial safety net — cash set aside for unexpected expenses or income loss.

## How Much to Save

- **Minimum target:** 3 months of essential expenses (rent/mortgage, bills, food, transport, debt payments).
- **Comfortable target:** 6 months of essential expenses.
- **Self-employed or variable income:** Aim for 6–12 months.
- **Single income household:** Aim for the higher end (6+ months).

## Where to Keep It

- Easy-access savings account (not locked away).
- Ideally in a Cash ISA for tax-free interest.
- Keep it separate from your spending account to avoid temptation.
- Consider splitting across 2 accounts: 1 month instant access, rest in a higher-rate notice account.

## What Counts as an Emergency

- Job loss or income reduction
- Essential car or home repairs
- Medical expenses
- Unexpected bills or fines
- Family emergencies

## What Does NOT Count

- Holidays or treats
- Sales or "good deals"
- Regular bills you forgot about (those should be budgeted)

## Building Your Fund

- Start with a £1,000 mini emergency fund if starting from zero.
- Automate monthly transfers — treat it like a bill.
- Use windfalls (tax refunds, bonuses) to accelerate.
- Once fully funded, redirect savings to investments or debt payoff.
